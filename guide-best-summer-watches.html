Seasonal Guide

Best Watches for Summer

January 2025 · 11 min read

Summer demands watches that handle pool days, beach trips, and spontaneous adventures without worry. The best summer watches combine water resistance, comfortable straps, and vibrant style that matches the season's energy.

What Makes a Great Summer Watch?

Summer watches need specific characteristics. Water resistance of at least 100m handles swimming and splashing. Rubber, silicone, or metal straps survive sweat and chlorine better than leather. Legibility in bright sunlight matters—some dials wash out, others remain clear. And summer is the time for color—why wear black when turquoise exists?

Dive Watches: The Summer Staple

Seiko Prospex "Turtle" (SRPG) — Around $400-500. The cushion-shaped case, 200m water resistance, and legendary reliability make the Turtle a summer icon. Available in countless colors, it's the enthusiast's affordable summer watch.

Orient Mako III — Around $300. In-house automatic movement, 200m water resistance, and excellent value. The Mako handles everything summer throws at it without anxiety about damaging an expensive piece.

Tudor Black Bay 58 — Around $3,700. If you want summer luxury without Rolex scarcity, the BB58's vintage proportions and 200m rating deliver. The blue dial version screams summer sophistication.

Omega Seamaster 300M — Around $5,400. The wave dial, ceramic bezel, and 300m rating combine style with serious capability. Omega's summer icon—Bond-approved for beach and beyond.

Colorful Options

Doxa SUB 200 — Around $1,000-1,400. The iconic orange dial is peak summer energy. Doxa's professional dive heritage means this isn't just colorful styling—it's genuine capability in an attention-grabbing package.

Oris Aquis Date (Various Colors) — Around $2,000-2,500. Oris offers the Aquis in cherry red, lake blue, and seafoam green—colors that embrace summer rather than hiding from it. 300m water resistance handles real diving.

Zodiac Super Sea Wolf — Around $1,300-1,500. Vibrant colors, genuine dive heritage, and distinctive vintage-inspired design. Zodiac embraces personality that conservative brands fear.

G-Shock: Ultimate Summer Durability

Casio G-Shock GA-2100 "Casioak" — Around $100-150. Essentially indestructible, water-resistant to 200m, and available in summer-appropriate colors. The slim profile doesn't scream "sports watch" but survives everything.

G-Shock Frogman (GWF-A1000) — Around $700. G-Shock's dedicated dive watch with ISO certification and asymmetrical case. For serious water activity, the Frogman handles conditions that would destroy other watches.

Elegant Summer Options

Cartier Santos — Around $7,000+. The integrated bracelet provides summer comfort, while 100m water resistance handles pool splashes. More elegant than typical summer sports watches, the Santos bridges vacation and evening dinner.

Rolex Datejust on Jubilee — Around $9,000+. The Datejust's 100m rating handles summer activities, and the Jubilee bracelet is famously comfortable in heat. Classic elegance that doesn't require babying.

Best Summer Straps

Rubber/Silicone: Ideal for summer. Waterproof, sweat-resistant, and comfortable in heat. Quick-dry and easy to clean.

NATO: Nylon dries quickly and comes in endless colors. Affordable enough to match different summer outfits. Some hate the bulk under the case.

Steel Bracelet: Handles water well, though can get hot in direct sun. More formal appearance than rubber.

Perlon: Lightweight woven synthetic that breathes well and dries quickly. Adjusts infinitely for perfect fit.

Avoid: Leather in summer. Sweat and water destroy leather quickly.

Water Resistance Reminder

100m handles swimming. 200m handles snorkeling and recreational diving. Don't push limits—water resistance degrades over time, and gaskets need replacement. If you're unsure, have water resistance tested before summer begins.

Summer Watch Care

Rinse watches with fresh water after ocean or pool exposure. Salt and chlorine are corrosive. Check that crowns are screwed down before swimming. Store watches away from direct sunlight when not wearing—UV degrades rubber and can fade dials over time.

Best Overall Summer Watch

The Seiko Prospex "Turtle" offers the best combination of capability, style, and worry-free pricing. For luxury summer wear, the Omega Seamaster 300M delivers everything with style that transitions from beach to dinner.
