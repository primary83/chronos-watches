The History of Nomos Glashütte

January 2025 · 9 min read

Less is more. Founded in 1990—just after German reunification allowed watchmaking to return to Glashütte—Nomos has become synonymous with Bauhaus-inspired minimalism and serious in-house manufacturing at accessible prices.

Post-Reunification Pioneer

When the Berlin Wall fell, the historic watchmaking town of Glashütte in eastern Germany was free to rebuild its industry after decades of Soviet control. Roland Schwertner founded Nomos in 1990, becoming one of the first new companies to establish there.

Starting from nothing, Nomos grew organically—no corporate backing, no luxury conglomerate parent. This independence shaped its character: straightforward, honest, substance over flash.

Bauhaus Design Philosophy

Nomos watches look different. Clean dials, often without indices. Minimalist typography. Restrained colors. The aesthetic draws from the Bauhaus design movement—where form follows function and ornamentation is stripped away.

This isn't styling for styling's sake. Each element serves a purpose. The result: watches that feel timeless rather than trendy, contemporary rather than dated.

In-House at This Price?

The remarkable thing about Nomos is its manufacture capability. The brand develops and produces its own movements—including the DUW calibers with Nomos's own escapement technology. Very few brands at this price point can claim true in-house movements.

Key Collections

  • Tangente: The original Nomos design since 1992
  • Orion: Pure minimalism, no indices
  • Club: More accessible entry point
  • Metro: Modern with power reserve display
  • Ahoi: Water-resistant sports collection

Glashütte Authenticity

To bear "Glashütte" on the dial, a watch must meet strict criteria—including having essential manufacturing steps performed in the town. Nomos not only meets these requirements but exceeds them, performing more production in Glashütte than the law requires.

For design-conscious buyers who want serious watchmaking without excessive decoration or inflated prices, Nomos offers a compelling proposition: German engineering, Bauhaus aesthetics, true manufacture capability.
