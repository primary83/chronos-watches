The History of Vacheron Constantin

January 2025 · 10 min read

One of the "Holy Trinity" of watchmaking. Since 1755, the oldest continuously operating watch manufacturer. Their motto: "Do better if possible, and that is always possible."

Jean-Marc Vacheron's Vision

In 1755, at age 24, Jean-Marc Vacheron established his watchmaking workshop in Geneva. This was the Age of Enlightenment, and Geneva was becoming the epicenter of fine watchmaking. Unlike many contemporaries who produced pocket watches in quantity, Vacheron focused on exceptional quality from the very beginning.

The first signed piece, dated September 17, 1755, marked the beginning of an unbroken tradition. Vacheron Constantin is the oldest watch manufacturer in continuous operation—never having stopped production in over 265 years, even through wars and economic upheavals.

François Constantin Partnership

In 1819, François Constantin joined the firm. A talented businessman with extensive travel experience, Constantin expanded the brand's reach across Europe and the Americas. His famous motto—"Do better if possible, and that is always possible"—became the company's guiding philosophy.

Constantin's commercial acumen combined with the technical expertise of Jacques-Barthélémi Vacheron (grandson of the founder) created a formidable partnership that elevated the brand to international prominence.

The Maltese Cross

In 1880, Vacheron Constantin adopted the Maltese Cross as its symbol—derived from a component in the movement that regulates mainspring tension. This cross has become one of the most recognized emblems in haute horlogerie, representing precision, craftsmanship, and heritage.

The symbol appears throughout Vacheron Constantin watches, from subtle case engravings to prominent positions on the Overseas collection's bezel.

Icons of Excellence

Throughout the 20th century, Vacheron Constantin created some of the world's most complicated timepieces. The 1929 "King Farouk" pocket watch featured an extraordinary number of complications. The 1950s brought elegant dress watches that defined post-war sophistication.

In 1972, the brand broke new ground with the 222—a luxury sports watch that predated both the Patek Philippe Nautilus and Audemars Piguet Royal Oak. Though not as commercially successful initially, it laid the groundwork for the beloved Overseas collection.

Key Collections

  • Overseas: The luxury sports watch with interchangeable straps
  • Patrimony: Ultra-thin dress watch perfection
  • Traditionnelle: Classical haute horlogerie
  • Historiques: Vintage designs reimagined
  • Fiftysix: Entry to Vacheron excellence
  • Les Cabinotiers: One-of-a-kind masterpieces

Les Cabinotiers

The name "Cabinotiers" refers to the artisan watchmakers who worked in the top floors of Geneva buildings in the 18th century, where large windows provided the best light. Today, Les Cabinotiers represents Vacheron Constantin's atelier for unique, one-of-a-kind creations.

This department creates the world's most complicated watches, including the Reference 57260—a pocket watch with 57 complications, the most ever made. Each Les Cabinotiers piece is unique, made to order for individual collectors.

The Holy Trinity

Vacheron Constantin stands alongside Patek Philippe and Audemars Piguet as the "Holy Trinity" of watchmaking. This distinction recognizes their unbroken history, technical innovation, and uncompromising quality standards.

What sets Vacheron apart is the sheer breadth of their expertise. From the thinnest movements to the most complicated mechanisms, from classic dress watches to luxury sports pieces, they have mastered every aspect of haute horlogerie.

Modern Excellence

Today, as part of the Richemont group since 1996, Vacheron Constantin continues to create some of the world's finest timepieces. The Geneva Seal on their movements ensures adherence to the strictest quality standards in Swiss watchmaking.

The Overseas collection has gained tremendous popularity as the sophisticated alternative in the luxury sports watch category. The Patrimony remains the benchmark for elegant dress watches. And Les Cabinotiers continues to push the boundaries of what's possible in watchmaking.

After more than 265 years, Vacheron Constantin proves that doing better is, indeed, always possible.
