Celebrity Watch Database 2026 — What Watches Do Famous People Wear?

March 25, 2026 · 15 min read
Home / Guides / Celebrity Watch Database 2026
← Back to Guides

Watches are the one luxury accessory that celebrities can wear anywhere — on the red carpet, courtside, during interviews, and on social media. Unlike other luxury items that come and go with fashion cycles, a watch on someone's wrist tells a story about their taste, their status, and sometimes their personality.

This database catalogs notable watch sightings from public figures across entertainment, sports, business, and politics. We focus on watches that have been reliably identified from public appearances, interviews, and verified sources — not speculation.

Note: Many celebrities own multiple watches and rotate their collection. The watches listed here represent notable sightings, not necessarily their only or primary timepiece. Estimated values reflect approximate market prices and may fluctuate.

Athletes

Basketball

LeBron James

One of the largest watch collections in professional sports. Known for wearing high-complication pieces and ultra-luxury brands courtside and during press conferences.

Notable pieces: Audemars Piguet Royal Oak Offshore · Patek Philippe Nautilus 5711 · Richard Mille RM 11-03 · Rolex Daytona "Rainbow" in Everose Gold

Collection estimated value: $2M+

Stephen Curry

Under Armour athlete and Omega brand ambassador since 2023. Frequently seen wearing Omega Seamaster and Speedmaster references during press events.

Notable pieces: Omega Seamaster Aqua Terra · Omega Speedmaster Moonwatch · Rolex Day-Date in yellow gold

Football (Soccer)

Cristiano Ronaldo

Arguably the most prolific celebrity watch collector. Known for spending millions on timepieces, with a preference for diamond-set and bespoke pieces from the top brands.

Notable pieces: Rolex GMT-Master II "Ice" · Franck Muller Cintrée Curvex · Jacob & Co. Astronomia Tourbillon · Bulgari Magsonic Sonnerie Tourbillon

Collection estimated value: $10M+

Lionel Messi

Jacob & Co. brand ambassador. Known for wearing statement pieces that match his understated personality with bold horological design.

Notable pieces: Jacob & Co. Epic X Chrono · Audemars Piguet Royal Oak · Patek Philippe Nautilus

Tennis

Roger Federer

Long-time Rolex brand ambassador and one of the most recognizable faces in watch advertising. Known for wearing Rolex during matches, press conferences, and public appearances.

Notable pieces: Rolex Datejust · Rolex Sky-Dweller · Rolex Yacht-Master · Rolex Day-Date

Rafael Nadal

Richard Mille ambassador since 2010. Famous for wearing the ultra-lightweight RM 027 (weighing only 20 grams) during matches, proving the durability of high-end watchmaking under extreme athletic conditions.

Notable pieces: Richard Mille RM 27-04 · Richard Mille RM 35-03

Individual pieces valued at $700K–$1M+

Actors and Entertainers

Brad Pitt

Breitling brand ambassador and genuine watch enthusiast. Known for wearing a diverse collection that spans luxury and tool watches, reflecting broader horological knowledge.

Notable pieces: Breitling Premier B01 Chronograph · Patek Philippe Ref. 5070 · Rolex Submariner (vintage)

John Mayer

Perhaps the most knowledgeable celebrity watch collector, known for his deep horological expertise. Has spoken extensively about watches on podcasts and was a contributing editor to Hodinkee. His collection represents serious connoisseurship.

Notable pieces: Patek Philippe 5711 "Tiffany Blue" · Rolex Daytona "Paul Newman" · Audemars Piguet Royal Oak "Jumbo" · A. Lange & Söhne Datograph

Collection estimated value: $20M+ (includes some of the rarest modern references)

Ryan Reynolds

Piaget brand ambassador with a preference for clean, understated luxury. Often seen in slimmer dress watches that complement his style.

Notable pieces: Piaget Altiplano · Omega Seamaster · Rolex Explorer

Jason Statham

Action star with a preference for tool watches that match his tough-guy image. Known for wearing Panerai's large-cased Luminor models.

Notable pieces: Panerai Luminor Marina · Rolex Submariner · IWC Big Pilot

Ellen DeGeneres

One of the most prominent female celebrity watch collectors. Known for an exceptional collection of Rolex and Patek Philippe references.

Notable pieces: Rolex Daytona "Rainbow" · Patek Philippe Nautilus · Rolex Day-Date

Musicians

Jay-Z

Serious collector with a preference for the highest tier of watchmaking. Known for wearing ultra-rare references and custom pieces.

Notable pieces: Patek Philippe Nautilus 5711/1A · Audemars Piguet Royal Oak · Richard Mille RM 056 · Hublot Big Bang

Ed Sheeran

Passionate and knowledgeable collector who regularly discusses watches on social media. Known for wearing his watches rather than treating them as display pieces.

Notable pieces: Patek Philippe Nautilus 5711 · A. Lange & Söhne 1815 · Audemars Piguet Royal Oak · Richard Mille RM 38-01

Drake

Known for customized and diamond-set luxury watches. Has commissioned bespoke pieces from Richard Mille and Jacob & Co.

Notable pieces: Richard Mille RM 69 · Jacob & Co. Astronomia · Patek Philippe Ref. 5980R

Business Leaders

Tim Cook (Apple CEO)

Naturally wears Apple Watch — typically the latest model with various band combinations. A walking advertisement for Apple's wearable technology.

Notable pieces: Apple Watch Ultra · Apple Watch Series (latest)

Bill Gates

Famous for wearing modest watches despite his wealth. Has been spotted wearing a Casio Duro — the $40 dive watch that appears in our Best Watches Under $300 guide.

Notable pieces: Casio MDV106 (Duro) · Casio digital watches

The most affordable celebrity watch collection on this list

Bernard Arnault (LVMH Chairman)

As the head of LVMH (which owns TAG Heuer, Hublot, Zenith, and Bulgari), Arnault naturally wears watches from his own portfolio.

Notable pieces: TAG Heuer Monaco · Hublot Big Bang · Zenith Chronomaster

What Celebrity Watch Choices Tell Us

Several patterns emerge from studying celebrity watch collections:

Rolex remains the universal luxury signal. Across all categories — athletes, entertainers, business leaders — Rolex appears more than any other brand. The Submariner and Daytona are the most common specific models.

Richard Mille dominates sports. The brand's ultra-lightweight construction and shock resistance make it genuinely practical for athletes who wear watches during competition. Rafael Nadal's tournament play while wearing an RM 027 is perhaps the most effective product placement in watch history.

Patek Philippe signals collector knowledge. When a celebrity wears Patek Philippe — especially a Nautilus 5711 or a complicated reference — it typically indicates deeper horological knowledge rather than simple brand shopping.

Modesty stands out. Bill Gates wearing a $40 Casio generates more media attention than another celebrity wearing a $500,000 Richard Mille — precisely because it's unexpected. The most memorable watch choices are often the most surprising ones.
