Best Watches for Plumbers & Electricians 2026

April 2026 · 11 min read

Skilled trades are the toughest environments for watches — plumbers submerge their hands constantly, electricians work near live circuits, and both professions involve tight spaces, tools, and physical labor that destroy delicate timepieces. The ideal tradesperson's watch is: cheap enough to replace after job-site damage, tough enough to survive daily abuse, and functional enough to track billable time and appointment schedules.

For Plumbers

Casio G-Shock DW-5600
$45–$55

200m water resistance handles full hand submersion in standing water, drain lines, and supply connections. Shock resistance survives wrench impacts and tight-space bumps. The countdown timer tracks billable hours per job. At $50, destroying it under a sink is a minor expense, not a catastrophe. The G-Shock is the plumber's standard for good reason.

Best for: Every plumber. Period.

For Electricians

Casio F-91W (Resin Case — Non-Conductive)
$10–$15

The F-91W's all-resin construction is non-conductive — critical for electricians who work near live circuits. Metal watches and metal bracelets can conduct electricity and cause burns or worse. The resin case and strap eliminate this risk entirely. At $12, it's the cheapest safety measure in an electrician's toolkit.

Best for: Electricians who need non-conductive material near live circuits.

The Tradesperson Watch Rule

Plumbers: G-Shock DW-5600 ($50) — waterproof and indestructible. Electricians: Casio F-91W ($12) — non-conductive resin saves lives. Both trades: never wear a metal bracelet watch on the job. The $12 Casio that keeps you safe is worth more than a $12,000 Rolex that puts you at risk.
