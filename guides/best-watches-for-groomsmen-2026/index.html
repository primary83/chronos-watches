Best Groomsmen Watch Gifts 2026 — Matching Style for the Wedding Party

April 2026 · 11 min read
Home / Guides / Best Groomsmen Watch Gifts 2026 — Matching Style for the Wedding Party
← Back to Guides

Groomsmen gifts should be useful, meaningful, and actually worn after the wedding — which eliminates most traditional options (nobody uses personalized flask sets). Watches check every box: they're worn daily, they commemorate the occasion, and they can be matching across the wedding party for photography. The key is budget — you're buying 3-7 watches, so per-unit cost matters.

Under $50 Per Groomsman

Casio MDV-106 "Duro" (Matching Set)
$35–$50 each

Buy 5 Duros for under $250 total. The dive-watch aesthetic photographs well in wedding pictures — matching Duros on groomsmen's wrists create a coordinated look that's subtle but intentional. The watches are nice enough that groomsmen will actually wear them after the wedding. On matching NATO straps in the wedding color ($10 each extra), they become personalized and thematic. Total for 5 groomsmen: ~$300. Best groomsmen gift value available.

Best for: Best value matching groomsmen set under $50/person.

Casio G-Shock DW-5600 (Matching Set)
$45–$55 each

Matching G-Shocks say "we survived the bachelor party and the wedding day." The DW-5600's iconic square design photographs well in group shots. Each watch includes a stopwatch for timing wedding speeches and an alarm for the rehearsal dinner. At $50 each, a set of 5 costs $250 — less than most individual groomsmen gift options. The G-Shocks will survive whatever the bachelor party throws at them and keep working for years after.

Best for: Indestructible matching groomsmen watches that survive the bachelor party.

$50–$150 Per Groomsman

Timex Weekender + Engraved Caseback
$40–$55 each (including engraving)

The Weekender on a leather NATO with "Groomsman — [Wedding Date] — [Name]" engraved on the caseback ($15-$20 for engraving). Each watch is personalized while matching the set. The interchangeable strap system means each groomsman can swap to their preferred color after the wedding. Total for 5 with engraving: ~$275. The engraving transforms a commodity watch into a personal keepsake.

Best for: Personalized groomsmen gifts with engraved casebacks.

Casio G-Shock GA-2100 "CasiOak" (Color Coordinated)
$90–$120 each

The CasiOak in matching colors — all black for black-tie weddings, earth tones for outdoor ceremonies, or each groomsman gets a different color for individuality. The CasiOak's design-forward aesthetic means groomsmen will actually want to wear these after the wedding. At $100 each, a set of 5 costs $500 — reasonable for a premium groomsmen gift that gets genuine daily use.

Best for: Style-conscious groomsmen who want something they'll actually wear.

$150–$300 Per Groomsman

Seiko 5 Sports SRPD (Matching)
$200–$260 each

For the groom who wants to give groomsmen their first "real" watch: matching Seiko 5 automatics with sweep seconds hands, exhibition casebacks, and day-date displays. The automatic movement makes each watch a conversation piece. At $230 each, a set of 5 costs ~$1,150 — premium territory for groomsmen gifts, but these are watches that groomsmen keep for decades. The Seiko 5 is the groomsmen gift that starts watch collections.

Best for: Premium groomsmen gifts that start watch collections.

The Groomsmen Gift Rule

The best groomsmen watch gift is the one they'll actually wear tomorrow, not just today. Casio Duro at $40/each for maximum value. CasiOak at $100/each for style-conscious guys. Seiko 5 at $230/each for the premium gesture. Include a card that says why you chose them to stand beside you — the card matters more than the watch, but the watch makes the card permanent.
