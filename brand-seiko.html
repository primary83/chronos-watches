About the Brand

Japanese Precision Since 1881

Seiko is Japan's most important watchmaker, founded in 1881 by Kintarō Hattori. The company revolutionized watchmaking in 1969 with the Astron — the world's first quartz wristwatch — triggering the 'quartz crisis.'

Seiko manufactures everything in-house: movements, cases, dials, hands, crystals, even lubricants. No other major watch brand achieves this level of vertical integration.

From the $50 Seiko 5 to the $5,000+ Presage line, Seiko offers genuine mechanical watchmaking at every price point. The NH35 and 4R movements power many of the world's most recommended affordable automatics.

Seiko is often the first brand recommended by experienced collectors for anyone entering the hobby — unmatched value at its price points.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Seiko a good watch brand?

+

Seiko is widely regarded as one of the best watch brands for value at any price point. It manufactures everything in-house and produces the most recommended affordable mechanical watches.

What is the best Seiko for beginners?

+

The Seiko Presage 'Cocktail Time' (~$300-$450) for stunning dial and versatility. The Seiko 5 (~$100-$250) for a first mechanical on a budget.

Is Seiko better than Citizen?

+

Seiko is preferred for mechanical watches; Citizen excels in solar-powered Eco-Drive technology. Both offer exceptional value. Seiko has a stronger enthusiast following.

What is the difference between Seiko and Grand Seiko?

+

Grand Seiko is Seiko's luxury division ($3,000-$50,000+) with hand-assembled movements rivaling Swiss haute horlogerie. Regular Seiko covers affordable to mid-range. They operate as separate brands.
