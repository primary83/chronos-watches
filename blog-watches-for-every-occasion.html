Style Guide

Best Watches for Every Occasion

January 2, 2025 · 9 min read

The right watch elevates every outfit and occasion. Whether you're closing a deal, diving into the ocean, or celebrating a milestone, there's a perfect timepiece for the moment. Here's your definitive guide.

While some collectors advocate for "one watch collections," most enthusiasts recognize that different occasions call for different tools. A rugged dive watch doesn't belong with black tie, and a delicate dress watch has no place on a construction site.

Understanding watch etiquette—and knowing which timepieces excel in each context—will ensure you're always appropriately dressed from the wrist up.

The Boardroom: Business & Professional

In professional settings, your watch should project competence and sophistication without screaming for attention. Think clean dials, moderate case sizes (38-42mm), and either leather straps or refined metal bracelets.

Key characteristics: Simple dial, no dive bezel, thin to medium case profile, classic colors (white, black, silver, blue).

Pro Tip

Match metal tones to your other accessories. Gold watch with gold cufflinks; steel watch with silver. Consistency signals attention to detail.

Black Tie: Formal Events & Galas

Traditional etiquette dictates a thin dress watch on a leather strap for formal occasions—or no watch at all. Modern interpretations allow more flexibility, but restraint remains key. Let your timepiece complement, not compete with, your attire.

Key characteristics: Thin case (under 10mm), small diameter (36-40mm), leather strap (black or dark brown), minimal complications, precious metal preferred.

The Beach & Beyond: Water Activities

Whether you're actually diving or simply want a watch that can handle pool parties and beach vacations, a proper dive watch offers peace of mind. Look for at least 100M water resistance for swimming; 200M+ for actual diving.

Key characteristics: Rotating bezel, screw-down crown, 200M+ water resistance, luminous markers, rubber or metal bracelet.

Weekend Casual: Everyday Wear

Your everyday watch should be versatile enough for errands, dinners, and weekend adventures. Sports watches with refined designs work perfectly—they handle physical activity while still looking sharp with a blazer.

Key characteristics: Versatile design, durable construction, comfortable bracelet or strap, 100M water resistance minimum.

Travel: Crossing Time Zones

For frequent travelers, a GMT watch is invaluable. These timepieces display multiple time zones simultaneously, letting you track home time while abroad. Essential for business travelers and globe-trotters alike.

Key characteristics: GMT complication, 24-hour bezel or subdial, legible dial, durable construction for transit.

Building a Versatile Collection

If you're building a collection, consider this three-watch strategy that covers virtually every situation:

1. A refined daily wear: Something like the Rolex Datejust or Omega Aqua Terra—dressy enough for meetings, durable enough for weekends.

2. A capable sports watch: A dive watch or GMT that handles active pursuits, travel, and casual occasions with ease.

3. A true dress watch: A thin, elegant piece on leather for formal events and special occasions.

With these three categories covered, you'll be appropriately equipped for any situation life presents.
