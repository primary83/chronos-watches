The History of Vulcain

January 2025 · 9 min read

The Cricket's distinctive chirp has reminded wearers of appointments since 1947. Vulcain's mechanical alarm watch became known as "The Presidents' Watch" after gracing the wrists of Truman, Eisenhower, Johnson, and Nixon. It remains the benchmark for alarm complications.

La Chaux-de-Fonds Origins

Maurice Ditisheim founded Vulcain in La Chaux-de-Fonds in 1858. The brand produced quality Swiss watches for decades before their defining innovation. Vulcain was respected but not exceptional—until the Cricket changed everything.

The Cricket is Born

In 1947, Vulcain introduced the Cricket—the first automatic wristwatch with mechanical alarm function. The movement's design produced a distinctive chirping sound audible despite the small case size. The Cricket could wake sleepers or remind wearers of appointments without external devices.

The Presidents' Watch

Vulcain gifted Crickets to US Presidents, earning the "Presidents' Watch" nickname. Harry Truman, Dwight Eisenhower, Lyndon Johnson, and Richard Nixon all wore Crickets. The White House association created marketing gold—no competitor could claim such distinguished users.

Golden Era

Through the 1950s and 1960s, Vulcain thrived. The Cricket became essential for business executives, travelers, and anyone needing reliable reminders. Variations included dive watches with alarms and dual-time zone versions for international travelers.

Quartz Crisis Impact

The quartz revolution devastated Vulcain. Digital watches offered alarms without mechanical complexity. Vulcain's specialty—mechanical alarm functionality—seemed obsolete. The brand declined severely.

Revival

Vulcain was revived in the 2000s under new ownership. The Cricket returned, updated for modern tastes while preserving its heritage. The mechanical alarm, once obsolete, became charming distinction in an era of smartphone alerts.

Modern Cricket

Today's Cricket maintains the essential character: set the alarm with a second crown, listen for the chirp at the appointed time. Modern versions include improved water resistance, contemporary sizes, and refined movements while keeping the signature sound.

Vulcain Today

For collectors seeking mechanical alarm complications with genuine heritage—and a connection to presidential history—Vulcain offers something unique. The Cricket's chirp remains distinctive, charming, and irreplaceable by digital alternatives.
