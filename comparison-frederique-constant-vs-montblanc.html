Comparison Guide

Frederique Constant vs Montblanc

Updated January 2026

← Back to Home

Frederique Constant and Montblanc overlap at the $2,000-$5,000 range. FC offers in-house movements at accessible prices; Montblanc brings Richemont luxury and writing instrument heritage. Different paths to Swiss watchmaking.

Quick Overview

Frederique Constant

  • Founded: 1988 (Geneva)
  • Owner: Citizen Group
  • Focus: Accessible Swiss luxury
  • Range: $895 - $5,000
  • Strengths: In-house at affordable prices

Montblanc

  • Founded: 1997 (watches)
  • Owner: Richemont
  • Focus: Luxury lifestyle extension
  • Range: $2,500 - $25,000+
  • Strengths: Brand prestige, design

Movement Philosophy

Frederique Constant develops their own movements in-house at remarkable price points. The FC-705 series competes with movements costing much more. Value-focused engineering.

Montblanc acquired Minerva manufacture and uses Sellita for entry pieces. Higher-end models feature genuine manufacture movements. Premium positioning, premium pricing.

Who Should Buy FC?

  • Value-conscious buyers wanting in-house movements
  • Those prioritizing specifications over brand recognition
  • Buyers wanting Swiss manufacture under $2,500

Who Should Buy Montblanc?

  • Those valuing brand prestige and recognition
  • Fans of the broader Montblanc ecosystem
  • Buyers wanting Richemont luxury positioning

Final Verdict

Buy FC for best specifications per dollar—in-house movements at accessible prices.

Buy Montblanc for brand prestige and luxury lifestyle association.
