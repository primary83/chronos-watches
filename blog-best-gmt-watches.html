Understanding GMT Watches

A GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) watch displays at least two time zones simultaneously. The typical setup includes a 24-hour hand that completes one rotation per day, paired with a rotating bezel marked 1-24. This allows you to track home time while the main hands show local time.

Types of GMT Movements

  • Caller GMT: The hour hand jumps in one-hour increments independently‚Äîideal for travelers who change zones frequently. You adjust local time without affecting the GMT hand.
  • Office GMT: The GMT hand can be set independently. Better for tracking a fixed second time zone (like your home or office) while traveling.
  • True GMT: Both the hour hand and GMT hand can be adjusted independently‚Äîmaximum flexibility but more complex movements.

üí° Which GMT Type Do You Need?

Frequent travelers benefit most from "Caller" GMTs where the hour hand jumps independently. If you mainly track one other time zone from home, an "Office" GMT works perfectly. For maximum flexibility, seek a "True" GMT, though these command premium prices.
