The History of Habring²

January 2025 · 9 min read

Habring² (pronounced "Habring squared") offers something almost mythical: genuine independent watchmaking at prices mere mortals can afford. This Austrian husband-wife team creates chronographs, jumping seconds, and split-seconds complications starting under €5,000.

The Habrings

Richard Habring spent 15 years at IWC, where he developed the legendary Doppelchronograph split-seconds mechanism. His wife Maria trained in watchmaking and business. In 2004, they established their own brand in Völkermarkt, Austria—far from Switzerland's traditional centers.

Austrian Independence

Operating from Austria provides advantages: lower costs than Switzerland, independence from Swiss industry politics, and distinctive identity. The Habrings leverage these benefits to offer exceptional value—complications that would cost multiples elsewhere.

Chronograph Excellence

Richard Habring's IWC experience shows in his chronographs. The Doppel series features split-seconds complications—typically found in watches costing €50,000+—at roughly €15,000. This isn't compromised quality but efficient production by experts who understand the mechanisms intimately.

The Felix

The Felix offers jumping seconds (dead-beat seconds) at entry-level prices. This complication—where the seconds hand jumps once per second rather than sweeping—typically appears in expensive pieces. Habring² democratized it.

Movement Approach

Habring² uses Valjoux and other established movements as bases, then modifies them extensively. This pragmatic approach—leveraging proven foundations while adding proprietary complications—enables quality at accessible prices without reinventing basics.

Hand Assembly

Every Habring² watch is assembled by the Habrings themselves or small trained team. This direct involvement ensures quality control impossible in larger operations. You're buying watches made by the people whose name is on the dial.

Erwin

The Erwin time-only model offers Habring² aesthetics and quality at even lower entry points. Simple doesn't mean compromised—the finishing and assembly standards remain consistent across the range.

Collector Recognition

Serious collectors increasingly recognize Habring² as exceptional value. Their watches appear in sophisticated collections alongside brands costing five times more—testament to quality that transcends price positioning.

Habring² Today

For collectors seeking independent watchmaking without six-figure prices—where expertise, complications, and genuine craft come at rational cost—Habring² represents perhaps the best value in serious horology.
