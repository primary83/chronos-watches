The History of H. Moser & Cie

January 2025 · 9 min read

H. Moser & Cie creates some of the most beautiful dials in watchmaking—their fumé gradients are instantly recognizable. But they're equally known for provocative stunts that mock industry pretensions. This is serious watchmaking that doesn't take itself too seriously.

Heinrich Moser's Legacy

Heinrich Moser founded his company in 1828 in Schaffhausen, Switzerland. He became one of the 19th century's most successful watchmakers, eventually establishing a watch empire that exported timepieces to Russia and beyond.

After Moser's death, the company eventually faded. The modern revival came in 2005 under the MELB Holding group.

Modern Revival

The revived H. Moser & Cie focused on what the original brand represented: understated excellence and genuine quality. Rather than chasing trends or flaunting logos, they created refined watches that let craftsmanship speak for itself.

CEO Edouard Meylan has shaped the brand's distinctive voice—confident enough to mock industry conventions while backing that attitude with genuine horological substance.

The Fumé Dial

H. Moser's fumé dials have become their signature. These graduated dials transition from darker edges to lighter centers (or vice versa), creating depth and visual interest that flat dials cannot match. Colors range from cosmic greens to ocean blues to fiery oranges.

The fumé technique requires multiple lacquer layers, each precisely applied and polished. Many brands now imitate the effect, but Moser's execution remains the standard.

Provocative Marketing

H. Moser gains attention through provocative stunts. They created a watch with a dial made from Swiss cheese to mock "Swiss Made" regulations. They produced a watch with no logo, no indices, and no hands to critique branding obsession. They've publicly criticized industry practices that other brands accept silently.

This isn't just marketing—it's genuine commentary from a brand confident enough to speak honestly.

In-House Excellence

Behind the provocations lies serious watchmaking. H. Moser produces in-house movements with hand-finished components, interchangeable escapements, and genuine manufacture depth. Their perpetual calendars, tourbillons, and minute repeaters compete at the highest levels.

Streamliner Collection

The Streamliner introduced H. Moser's integrated bracelet sports watch—their response to the category dominated by Nautilus and Royal Oak. The distinctive cushion case and flowing bracelet created something genuinely new rather than derivative.

Endeavour Collection

The Endeavour represents classical H. Moser—refined dress watches showcasing fumé dials in their purest form. These understated pieces prove that true luxury whispers rather than shouts.

Pioneer Collection

The Pioneer offers sportier designs with greater water resistance while maintaining Moser's aesthetic identity. These versatile pieces suit more active lifestyles.

H. Moser Today

H. Moser & Cie offers a rare combination: genuine haute horlogerie capability, stunning dial artistry, and willingness to challenge industry pretensions. For collectors who want substance with personality—beautiful watches from a brand unafraid to speak its mind—H. Moser delivers distinction.
