The History of Eberhard & Co

January 2025 · 9 min read

Eberhard & Co bridges Swiss manufacture with Italian passion. Founded in 1887 and now Italian-owned, the brand offers genuine chronograph heritage—including split-seconds complications—at prices below similarly equipped Swiss competitors.

Swiss Origins

Georges-Lucien Eberhard founded Eberhard & Co in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland, in 1887. The brand established reputation for quality chronographs and complicated watches, earning recognition at international exhibitions and among discriminating buyers.

Italian Connection

Since the mid-20th century, Eberhard has had strong Italian ties. The brand is now Italian-owned while maintaining Swiss manufacture. This combination creates unique character—Italian design sensibility with Swiss mechanical credibility.

Tazio Nuvolari

The Tazio Nuvolari collection honors the legendary Italian racing driver. These chronographs feature motorsport-inspired design with substantial cases and legible displays. The connection to racing heritage feels authentic given Nuvolari's historical significance.

Chrono 4

The Chrono 4 features four subdials arranged in a row—a distinctive display that earned patents and design awards. This unusual layout demonstrates willingness to innovate beyond conventional chronograph presentation.

Split-Seconds Heritage

Eberhard has produced split-seconds (rattrapante) chronographs throughout its history. This complicated function—allowing timing of two simultaneous events—typically appears only in expensive watches. Eberhard offers it at relatively accessible prices.

Extra-Fort

The Extra-Fort collection offers classical chronograph aesthetics with historical references. These pieces appeal to collectors wanting traditional design without fashion-brand associations or entry-level compromises.

Value Position

Eberhard occupies interesting market position: genuine heritage, Swiss manufacture, Italian ownership, competitive pricing. For chronograph enthusiasts, this combination offers substantial value versus better-known brands with similar technical content.

Eberhard Today

For collectors seeking chronograph heritage with Italian flair—where racing connection and split-seconds expertise come without luxury brand premiums—Eberhard & Co delivers overlooked excellence.
