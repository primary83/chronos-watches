Best Pilot & Aviation Watches 2025

12 min read Updated January 2025

Pilot watches represent one of the most storied categories in watchmaking. Born from the necessity of early aviation, these timepieces have evolved from essential flight instruments to iconic symbols of adventure and precision. Whether you're an actual pilot or simply appreciate the heritage and functionality, here are the best aviation watches available today.

What Makes a True Pilot Watch?

Authentic pilot watches share several key characteristics developed through decades of aviation history:

  • High legibility — Large, clear numerals readable at a glance
  • Anti-magnetic properties — Protection from cockpit instruments
  • Oversized crown — Operable with flight gloves
  • GMT/dual time — Essential for crossing time zones
  • Slide rule bezel — For flight calculations (on some models)
  • Luminous hands and markers — Visibility in dark cockpits

Top Pilot Watches

IWC Big Pilot's Watch 43

$9,500

The IWC Big Pilot is the quintessential modern pilot watch. Its 43mm case (down from the original 46mm) offers the iconic design in a more wearable size. The conical crown, originally designed for use with flight gloves, is instantly recognizable.

Case: 43mm Steel
Movement: IWC 82100
Power Reserve: 60 hours
Water Resistance: 100m

Breitling Navitimer B01 Chronograph 43

$9,600

The Navitimer is arguably the most famous pilot's watch ever made. Its slide rule bezel allows pilots to perform essential flight calculations. Now powered by Breitling's excellent in-house B01 movement.

Case: 43mm Steel
Movement: Breitling B01
Power Reserve: 70 hours
Water Resistance: 30m

Longines Spirit Zulu Time

$3,300

Exceptional value in a GMT pilot watch. The Spirit Zulu Time features COSC-certified chronometer accuracy, a true GMT movement, and styling inspired by Longines' aviation heritage. One of the best values in the category.

Case: 42mm Steel
Movement: L844.4 (GMT)
Power Reserve: 72 hours
Water Resistance: 100m

Bell & Ross BR 03-92

$4,300

Designed to look like a cockpit instrument, the BR 03-92 takes its square case directly from aviation gauge aesthetics. High legibility and unmistakable presence make it a modern classic.

Case: 42mm Steel (Square)
Movement: BR-CAL.302
Power Reserve: 54 hours
Water Resistance: 100m

Sinn 104 St Sa

$1,890

German engineering at an accessible price. Sinn builds watches for actual pilots, and the 104 distills that expertise into a versatile everyday pilot watch with day-date functionality.

Case: 41mm Steel
Movement: SW 220-1
Power Reserve: 38 hours
Water Resistance: 200m

Budget-Friendly Options

Seiko Prospex Alpinist SPB117

$725

While technically a field watch, the Alpinist's compass bezel and explorer heritage appeal to aviation enthusiasts. Exceptional value with Seiko's reliable 6R35 movement.

Case: 39.5mm Steel
Movement: Seiko 6R35
Power Reserve: 70 hours
Water Resistance: 200m

Hamilton Khaki Aviation Pilot Day Date

$995

Hamilton's aviation heritage runs deep, and the Khaki Aviation delivers authentic pilot watch styling with Swiss reliability at an exceptional price point.

Case: 42mm Steel
Movement: H-40
Power Reserve: 80 hours
Water Resistance: 100m

Choosing the Right Pilot Watch

Consider these factors when selecting your aviation timepiece:

  • Actual pilots — Prioritize legibility, GMT function, and anti-magnetic properties
  • Heritage enthusiasts — Look to IWC, Breitling, and Longines for authentic history
  • Everyday wear — Consider 40-43mm cases for versatility
  • Budget conscious — Hamilton and Seiko offer genuine quality at accessible prices

Pro Tip

If you're an actual pilot, check your airline's regulations. Some airlines have specific requirements about watch types allowed in the cockpit, particularly regarding smartwatches and potential interference.

Our Recommendation

For most buyers, the Longines Spirit Zulu Time offers the best combination of heritage, functionality, and value. If budget allows, the IWC Big Pilot's Watch 43 is the definitive modern pilot watch.
