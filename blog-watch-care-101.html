Watch Care 101: A Beginner's Guide

January 2025 · 8 min read

Whether you've just purchased your first luxury watch or received a treasured family heirloom, proper watch care is essential. A well-maintained timepiece can last generations, while neglect can lead to costly repairs.

Why Watch Care Matters

A mechanical watch contains hundreds of tiny components working in precise harmony. Dust, moisture, oils from your skin, and everyday wear can affect performance over time. Regular care protects your investment and ensures accurate timekeeping for years.

Daily Watch Care Essentials

Wiping Down Your Watch

At the end of each day, gently wipe your watch with a soft, lint-free cloth. This removes sweat, oils, and dirt that accumulate during wear. Pay attention to the case back and between the lugs.

Avoiding Extreme Temperatures

Sudden temperature changes can affect accuracy and damage gaskets. Avoid wearing your watch in saunas, hot tubs, or leaving it in direct sunlight.

Keeping Away from Magnets

Magnets are everywhere—phones, tablets, laptop speakers, handbag clasps. Strong magnetic fields can magnetize the hairspring, causing your watch to run fast or erratically. Keep 5cm distance from magnetic sources.

Quick Tip: The 5-5-5 Rule

Wipe your watch for 5 seconds, check the crown is pushed in (5-second check), and store it 5 inches from electronics.

Understanding Water Resistance

30m (3 ATM): Splash resistant only. Safe for hand washing and light rain.

50m (5 ATM): Suitable for swimming in shallow water.

100m (10 ATM): Safe for swimming, snorkeling, and recreational water activities.

200m+ (20 ATM): True dive watches. Suitable for scuba diving.

Storing Your Watch Properly

Store your watch in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. A dedicated watch box with soft lining is ideal. Extreme humidity can cause moisture damage, while very dry conditions can cause leather straps to crack.

Daily Watch Care Checklist

  • Wipe down with soft cloth after wearing
  • Check that crown is fully pushed in/screwed down
  • Store in watch box or soft pouch
  • Keep away from magnets and electronics
  • Avoid spraying cologne/perfume directly on watch
  • Remove before strenuous activities

When to Seek Professional Help

If your watch is running significantly fast or slow, making unusual sounds, fogging under the crystal, or if the crown feels loose, it's time to visit a professional watchmaker. Most mechanical watches should be serviced every 3-5 years.

Your watch is more than a timekeeping device. It's a precision instrument and often a meaningful possession. Treat it with care, and it will serve you faithfully for decades.
