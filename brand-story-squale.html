The History of Squale

January 2025 · 9 min read

Before Squale made their own watches, they made dive watch cases for other brands—including some of the most famous names in Swiss watchmaking. That expertise in waterproof case construction gives Squale dive watches credibility that marketing alone cannot create.

Italian Case Making Heritage

Squale was founded in 1946 by Charles von Büren in Switzerland, though the brand developed strong Italian connections. The company specialized in manufacturing dive watch cases, developing expertise in the waterproof construction essential for underwater timepieces.

The name "Squale" means "shark" in Italian and French—an appropriate name for a company focused on dive watches.

Supplying the Industry

Squale became a significant supplier of dive watch cases to Swiss brands. When you see vintage dive watches from various manufacturers, there's a reasonable chance Squale made the case. This OEM heritage means Squale understood dive watch construction at a fundamental level.

Making cases for demanding Swiss brands required meeting exacting standards—experience that informed Squale's own watches.

Professional Diver Use

Squale watches were used by professional divers, military units, and underwater workers who needed reliable dive instruments. These weren't fashion accessories—they were working tools for people whose safety depended on reliable equipment.

The 1521 Collection

The Squale 1521 represents the brand's core offering—a professional dive watch rated to 500 meters with classic styling. The 1521's design draws from Squale's heritage models, offering vintage aesthetics with modern reliability.

Available in various dial colors and bezel configurations, the 1521 provides genuine dive capability at accessible prices.

50 Atmos

The 50 Atmos collection honors Squale's heritage of 500-meter water resistance. These watches feature the cushion-shaped cases characteristic of vintage Squale divers, attracting collectors who appreciate 1960s and 1970s dive watch aesthetics.

Master Collection

The Master collection offers enhanced specifications for serious divers—greater water resistance, robust construction, and professional-grade features for actual underwater use.

Swiss Movement, Italian Style

Modern Squale watches combine Swiss movements with design sensibility influenced by Italian style. This blend creates dive watches with personality that pure Swiss functionality sometimes lacks.

Von Büren Family

Squale remains connected to its founding heritage, maintaining the focus on dive watches that defined the company from its origins. This consistency prevents brand dilution into categories where Squale has no expertise.

Collector Appeal

Vintage Squale watches have developed collector followings, with original models commanding significant prices. Modern Squale production offers similar aesthetics and genuine capability at accessible prices.

Squale Today

Squale offers legitimate dive watch heritage—not styling inspired by dive watches, but actual capability built by a company that spent decades making cases for the industry's best. For dive watch enthusiasts seeking value with genuine credentials, Squale delivers authentic underwater heritage.
