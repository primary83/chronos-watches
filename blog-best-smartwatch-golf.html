What Golf Features Matter?

Modern golf watches offer far more than just yardage. Here's what to look for:

GPS Distance

The fundamental feature. Accurate front/center/back distances to the green, plus distances to hazards, layup points, and doglegs. Look for watches with 43,000+ preloaded courses (Garmin) to ensure your home course is included.

Green View / Pin Placement

Move the pin position on a digital green map to get accurate distance to today's actual flag location. Essential for approach shots and club selection.

Hazard Information

See distances to bunkers, water, and out-of-bounds areas. Better watches show layup distances to carry hazards safely.

Shot Tracking

Automatic or manual tracking of each shot. Know exactly how far you hit each club. Some watches auto-detect shots using accelerometers; others require manual tagging.

Slope/Elevation

"Plays like" distances that account for uphill/downhill shots. Note: Slope features are typically tournament-illegal—look for watches with "tournament mode" that disables them.

Tournament Legal: For competitive play, ensure your watch has a mode that disables slope calculations and advice features. Garmin watches include "Big Numbers Mode" that shows only basic distances, making them legal for tournament use.

What About Apple Watch & Galaxy Watch?

General-purpose smartwatches can work for golf with third-party apps, but they're not the ideal choice:

Pros: One watch for everything, notifications during rounds, music playback
Cons: Shorter battery life, app subscriptions required, less golf-specific optimization, smaller course databases

If golf is your primary sport and you play frequently, a dedicated golf watch like Garmin Approach will serve you better. If you play occasionally and want one watch for all activities, Apple Watch or Galaxy Watch with a golf app is a reasonable compromise.

The Bottom Line

For serious golfers, the Garmin Approach S70 is the best overall choice with unmatched golf features and full smartwatch functionality. The Approach S42 hits the sweet spot of value—all essential features at $299. Budget-conscious players can start with the Approach S12 at $199. And golfers who want one watch for everything should consider Apple Watch Ultra 2 with a golf app subscription.
