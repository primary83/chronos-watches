Nomos vs Junghans: German Bauhaus Battle

Last Updated: January 2026 | Reading Time: 11 minutes

German watchmaking offers compelling alternatives to Swiss dominance, with Nomos and Junghans representing distinct approaches to Bauhaus-inspired design. Both brands embrace minimalist aesthetics rooted in German design tradition, yet their manufacturing philosophies, movement strategies, and market positioning differ significantly.

Nomos Glashütte

Price Range: $1,700 - $4,500 | Founded: 1990 | Location: Glashütte, Germany

Nomos represents modern German watchmaking independence. The brand manufactures their own movements in-house—unusual at their price point. The Alpha and DUW calibers demonstrate genuine horological capability. Glashütte location provides access to the region's watchmaking heritage and skilled workforce.

Design philosophy emphasizes pure Bauhaus minimalism with distinctive hands and typography. Each collection offers cohesive aesthetic vision executed with exceptional finishing quality. The higher price point reflects in-house movement manufacture and superior execution.

Junghans

Price Range: $400 - $2,500 | Founded: 1861 | Location: Schramberg, Germany

Junghans brings 160+ years of heritage, including the iconic Max Bill collection designed by the Bauhaus master himself. The brand primarily uses Swiss ETA and Sellita movements, focusing resources on design excellence rather than movement manufacture.

The Max Bill line remains definitive Bauhaus watch design—pure, functional, timeless. More accessible pricing enables broader market reach. Quartz and automatic options span diverse preferences and budgets.

Key Differences

Movement Philosophy: Nomos manufactures in-house; Junghans uses Swiss movements.

Price Positioning: Nomos targets enthusiast collectors; Junghans offers accessible design excellence.

Heritage: Junghans claims 160+ years; Nomos represents modern independent watchmaking.

Design Focus: Both embrace Bauhaus minimalism with distinct interpretations.

Verdict

Choose Nomos for in-house movement prestige, superior finishing, and modern Glashütte credentials. Choose Junghans for Max Bill's authentic Bauhaus design, accessible pricing, and heritage depth. Both deliver German design excellence—budget and movement preferences determine the better choice.
