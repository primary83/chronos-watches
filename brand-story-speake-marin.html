The History of Speake-Marin

January 2025 · 9 min read

Peter Speake-Marin represents a rare breed: a British watchmaker who trained in Switzerland, mastered restoration, then created his own brand. His distinctive Piccadilly case and topping tool logo mark watches that blend English sensibility with Swiss craftsmanship.

British Watchmaker, Swiss Training

Peter Speake-Marin trained at watchmaking schools in England and Switzerland before spending years at Somlo Antiques in London restoring important historical pieces. This education—learning by disassembling and reassembling masterworks—provided deep understanding of what makes exceptional watches.

The Topping Tool

Speake-Marin's logo depicts a "topping tool"—a vintage watchmaking instrument used to cut gear teeth. This obscure reference signals respect for traditional craft and identifies those who recognize it as fellow enthusiasts.

Founding the Brand

Speake-Marin established his brand in 2002, creating watches in Switzerland that reflected his restoration experience and personal aesthetic. Early pieces demonstrated technical competence and distinctive design language that attracted collector attention.

The Piccadilly Case

The Piccadilly case—named for London's famous circus—became Speake-Marin's signature. Its cushion shape with distinctive lugs creates immediately recognizable profile. The case appears across collections, providing brand coherence.

Movement Development

Speake-Marin developed proprietary movements including the SMA02, allowing independence from standard Swiss ébauches. In-house capability demonstrated serious watchmaking ambition beyond design and assembly.

The One & Two Collections

The One & Two collections offered accessible entry to the brand—refined designs, quality movements, distinctive aesthetics at prices below flagship complications. These pieces introduced Speake-Marin to collectors who might later pursue rarer models.

Ownership Evolution

The brand has evolved through various ownership and business structures. Peter Speake-Marin himself stepped back from day-to-day operations, though his design influence continues. This transition—common in independent watchmaking—presents both challenges and opportunities.

Speake-Marin Today

For collectors seeking independent watchmaking with British character and Swiss execution—where historical awareness meets contemporary design—Speake-Marin offers distinctive proposition among crowded independent landscape.
