The History of Omega

December 2024 · 11 min read

First watch on the Moon. Official Olympic timekeeper. James Bonds choice. Omega has written history on wrists around the world and beyond.

La Chaux-de-Fonds Origins

Louis Brandt founded the company in 1848. The Omega name came in 1894 with a revolutionary movement—the Omega caliber—that was so precise it earned the companys new name.

The Moonwatch

In 1969, Buzz Aldrin wore his Speedmaster on the Moon during Apollo 11. The Moonwatch has accompanied astronauts on every NASA crewed mission since, cementing its legendary status.

Key Collections

  • Speedmaster: The Moonwatch
  • Seamaster: Dive watch excellence
  • Constellation: Precision chronometer
  • De Ville: Elegant dress watches

Today, as part of the Swatch Group, Omega continues to innovate with Co-Axial escapements and Master Chronometer certification.
