The History of Anordain

January 2025 · 8 min read

Anordain produces enamel dials in-house in Glasgow—a craft that even most luxury Swiss brands outsource. Each dial is fired multiple times at 800°C, creating luminous colors and depth that printed dials cannot match. This is artisanal watchmaking from an unexpected place.

Glasgow Origins

Anordain was founded in 2018 by Lewis Heath in Glasgow, Scotland—far from traditional watchmaking centers. The company name comes from the Scots Gaelic word meaning "to arrange" or "to ordain," reflecting the careful craftsmanship involved in creating enamel dials.

Establishing a watch brand in Scotland was unconventional; establishing one focused on enamel dial production was almost unheard of.

The Enamel Difference

Enamel dials are created by fusing powdered glass to metal at extremely high temperatures. The process requires multiple firings, each risking the dial's destruction. This labor-intensive technique produces colors with depth and luminosity that paint or lacquer cannot replicate.

Most watch brands have abandoned enamel work or outsource it to specialized workshops. Anordain brought the process in-house, controlling quality and enabling experimentation.

Fumé Dials

Anordain's fumé dials feature gradients that shift from lighter centers to darker edges. These subtle color transitions demonstrate mastery of enamel technique, as controlling color across the firing process requires exceptional skill.

Model 1

The Model 1 showcases Anordain's enamel expertise in a clean, classic case design. Available in various enamel colors, each Model 1 is visually unique due to the handmade nature of the dial production.

Model 2

The Model 2 offers a slightly different case shape while maintaining the enamel dial focus. Different colorways and configurations provide options while keeping the emphasis on dial craftsmanship.

Field Watches

Anordain expanded into field watches with enamel dials, bringing their artisanal approach to more rugged designs. This demonstrated that enamel work wasn't limited to dress watches.

Production Reality

Enamel dial production limits output. Anordain cannot mass-produce watches because each dial requires individual attention through multiple firings. This constraint ensures quality but creates waitlists and limited availability.

Scottish Manufacturing

Anordain assembles watches in Glasgow, with dials produced on-site. While movements are sourced from Switzerland, the critical dial work happens in Scotland, giving the brand genuine local craft credentials.

Growing Recognition

Watch enthusiasts have discovered Anordain's distinctive offering, creating demand that exceeds supply. The brand has earned respect for bringing nearly-lost craftsmanship to accessible price points.

Anordain Today

Anordain proves that meaningful watchmaking can emerge from unexpected places. For buyers seeking genuine artisanal craft—dials made by hand using centuries-old techniques—Anordain offers something that even prestigious Swiss brands typically cannot: in-house enamel work at attainable prices.
