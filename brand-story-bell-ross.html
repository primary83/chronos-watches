The History of Bell & Ross

January 2025 · 9 min read

Look at a Bell & Ross BR 01 and you see something familiar—a cockpit instrument. That's exactly the point. Founded in 1992, Bell & Ross has built its identity around creating watches inspired by aviation instrumentation.

The Beginning

Bruno Belamich (the "Bell") and Carlos Rosillo (the "Ross") met at university and shared a passion for aviation and military equipment. They founded Bell & Ross in Paris with a clear mission: create professional watches meeting the exact specifications of pilots, astronauts, and special forces.

Early models were based on Sinn cases and movements—the German tool watch brand with expertise in aviation watches. This partnership provided instant credibility and technical capability.

The Square Icon

In 2005, Bell & Ross introduced the BR 01—a 46mm square watch that looked like it had been lifted directly from an aircraft instrument panel. The circular dial within a square case, four visible screws, and ultra-legible typography became instantly iconic.

The design wasn't arbitrary. Each element referenced real cockpit instruments: the round dial in square housing, the screw-mounted bezel, the anti-reflective coating. Form followed function, then became fashion.

Professional Credentials

Bell & Ross watches are designed to meet real professional standards. The brand has worked with the French Air Force, elite diving units, and space agencies. Each collection addresses specific professional needs: pilots, divers, bomb disposal units.

Key Collections

  • BR 01: The original 46mm cockpit instrument
  • BR 03: 42mm "urban" version of the icon
  • BR 05: Integrated bracelet sports watch
  • BR-X1: Skeletonized chronograph complications
  • Diver: Professional diving instruments

Chanel Partnership

Since 2018, Bell & Ross has been majority-owned by Chanel, providing resources for independent operation while maintaining the brand's tool-watch identity. The partnership has enabled investments in manufacture capabilities.

For pilots, aviation enthusiasts, and those who appreciate purpose-driven design, Bell & Ross offers something distinctive: watches that look like instruments because they're designed to function as instruments.
