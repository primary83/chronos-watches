Hublot vs Rolex: The Complete Comparison

Updated February 2026 · 15 min read

Quick Overview

Hublot

  • Founded: 1980 (Switzerland)
  • Price Range: $8,000 - $100,000+
  • Value Retention: 40-60%
  • Philosophy: Art of Fusion
  • Group: LVMH
  • Signature: Big Bang, exotic materials

Rolex

  • Founded: 1905 (London)
  • Price Range: $7,000 - $75,000+
  • Value Retention: 95-100%+
  • Philosophy: Perfected tradition
  • Group: Independent
  • Signature: Submariner, Daytona, Datejust

Hublot and Rolex represent opposite ends of luxury watch philosophy. Hublot embraces "The Art of Fusion"—exotic materials and bold designs. Rolex perfects tradition—refining proven designs with obsessive detail. One is loud; the other is quiet. Both are unmistakably luxury.

Heritage & History

Hublot

Carlo Crocco founded Hublot in 1980 with a radical idea: a gold watch on a rubber strap. Under Jean-Claude Biver from 2004, the Big Bang catapulted Hublot to fame with bold fusion of materials. Hublot partners with FIFA, Ferrari, and the Premier League.

Rolex

Hans Wilsdorf founded Rolex in 1905 and spent over a century perfecting the luxury tool watch. The Oyster case (1926), Submariner (1953), and Daytona (1963) defined entire categories. Rolex's evolutionary approach produces the most recognized watch brand on Earth.

Winner: Rolex — incomparable heritage spanning over a century

Pro Tip

Hublot's dramatic depreciation is both risk and opportunity. Pre-owned Big Bangs with the UNICO chronograph can be found at 40-50% of retail—genuine bargains for those who love the design. Just don't buy Hublot as an investment.

Key Models Compared

Big Bang vs Submariner

The Big Bang ($15,000-$21,000) is bold with exotic materials and the UNICO flyback chronograph. The Submariner ($9,100) is the most iconic dive watch ever made. The Big Bang makes a louder visual statement; the Submariner makes a louder financial one.

Who Should Buy Hublot?

  • Bold, attention-grabbing design excites you
  • Material innovation (sapphire cases, Magic Gold) appeals
  • You buy watches to wear, not invest
  • Motorsport and football partnerships appeal

Who Should Buy Rolex?

  • Investment value and resale matter
  • Timeless design that never dates is preferred
  • 120+ years of heritage is important
  • You want the most universally respected brand

Final Verdict

Buy Hublot if you want a bold, modern luxury watch that pushes material boundaries. Accept the depreciation as the cost of wearing something truly different.

Buy Rolex for the gold standard—proven designs, exceptional movements, unmatched resale, and universal recognition.

Rolex wins on paper; Hublot wins on personality. Head vs heart.
