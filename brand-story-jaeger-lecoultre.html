The History of Jaeger-LeCoultre

January 2025 · 11 min read

Known as "the watchmaker's watchmaker," Jaeger-LeCoultre has supplied movements to the most prestigious brands in history. From the Vallee de Joux, this Grande Maison has created over 1,200 calibers since 1833.

The Vallee de Joux

Antoine LeCoultre founded his workshop in 1833 in Le Sentier, in the remote Vallee de Joux. This isolated valley became the cradle of Swiss watchmaking, where the harsh winters drove farmers to develop precision craftsmanship.

The Reverso Legend

Created in 1931 for polo players who needed to protect their watch faces, the Reverso's swiveling case became an icon of Art Deco design. Today, it remains one of the most recognizable watches in the world.

Movement Mastery

Jaeger-LeCoultre has created movements for Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, and Vacheron Constantin. The Caliber 101, created in 1929, remains the world's smallest mechanical movement.

Key Collections

  • Reverso: The iconic swiveling case
  • Master: Classic round complications
  • Polaris: Sports diving heritage
  • Atmos: The perpetual clock
  • Duometre: Dual-wing precision

Today, Jaeger-LeCoultre continues to innovate while honoring its heritage, creating everything from ultra-thin dress watches to complex astronomical complications—all from the same valley where it began nearly 200 years ago.
