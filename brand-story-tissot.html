The History of Tissot

January 2025 · 10 min read

"Innovators by Tradition." Since 1853, Tissot has been making Swiss quality accessible. As part of the Swatch Group, Tissot brings genuine Swiss watchmaking to millions—often serving as the first Swiss watch for new enthusiasts.

Le Locle Origins

Charles-Félicien Tissot and his son Charles-Émile founded Tissot in 1853 in Le Locle, Switzerland. From the beginning, they looked beyond Swiss borders, establishing early exports to America and Russia. This global vision distinguished Tissot from more insular competitors.

A History of Firsts

Tissot has accumulated an impressive list of innovations: the first mass-produced pocket watch (1853), first anti-magnetic watch (1930), first plastic watch (1971), and first tactile watch with touch-screen crystal (1999). Innovation isn't marketing speak here—it's documented history.

Sports Timing Heritage

Tissot is the official timekeeper for numerous sports: NBA basketball, MotoGP racing, cycling, fencing, and more. This role demands precision under pressure and has driven technical development, particularly in chronograph accuracy.

Key Collections

  • PRX: The iconic 1970s integrated bracelet, revived
  • Gentleman: Versatile everyday dress watch
  • T-Touch: Touch-screen technology pioneer
  • Seastar: Capable dive watches
  • Le Locle: Classic dress watch elegance

The PRX Phenomenon

In 2021, Tissot reissued the PRX—a 1970s design with integrated bracelet similar in style to the Royal Oak and Nautilus. Priced under $700, it brought the "luxury sports watch" aesthetic to an entirely new audience. The watch became a social media sensation and gateway to collecting.

Gateway to Swiss Watches

For many collectors, Tissot represents the first step into Swiss watchmaking. The brand offers genuine Swiss movements, quality construction, and respectable heritage—all at prices competing with fashion watches that offer none of those things.

In a world where entry-level "luxury" keeps climbing higher, Tissot proves Swiss quality doesn't require a four-figure budget.
