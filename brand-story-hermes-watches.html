The History of Hermès Watches

January 2025 · 9 min read

Hermès brings 180+ years of luxury craftsmanship to watchmaking. Their approach differs from most fashion houses: serious Swiss manufacture investment, distinctive design language rooted in equestrian heritage, and genuine horological ambition.

Leather First

Hermès began as harness and saddle maker in 1837, establishing expertise in leather craft that remains central to their identity. When they entered watchmaking, straps became obvious differentiator—no one makes leather like Hermès.

Watch History

Hermès produced watches from the 1920s, initially sourcing from Swiss manufacturers. For decades, watches were accessories complementing their leather goods—beautiful objects without distinctive horological identity.

La Montre Hermès

In 1978, Hermès established La Montre Hermès in Biel/Bienne, Switzerland—dedicated watch subsidiary with proper Swiss manufacture credentials. This signaled commitment beyond simple licensing or outsourcing.

Cape Cod

Henri d'Origny designed the Cape Cod in 1991—an anchor-shaped case with distinctive proportions. It became Hermès's signature watch design, particularly popular with the wraparound "Double Tour" strap.

Vaucher Partnership

Hermès acquired 25% stake in Vaucher Manufacture, gaining access to high-quality movements and manufacturing capability. This investment demonstrated serious horological intent beyond fashion brand expectations.

Arceau

The Arceau, designed in 1978, features asymmetric lugs referencing stirrups—equestrian heritage made subtle. Various complications have appeared in Arceau cases, including moon phases and perpetual calendars.

In-House Movements

Hermès developed proprietary complications including the "Temps Suspendu" (suspended time) mechanism and various métiers d'art collaborations. These aren't standard movements with fashion brand branding but genuine technical developments.

Slim d'Hermès

The Slim d'Hermès offers contemporary ultra-thin dress watch with in-house automatic movement. Its typography, designed specifically for Hermès, demonstrates attention to detail matching their broader design philosophy.

Strap Excellence

Hermès straps remain unmatched. Their leather expertise translates directly—exotic skins, perfect finishing, proprietary tanning processes. Many customers buy Hermès watches specifically for strap options unavailable elsewhere.

Hermès Watches Today

Hermès occupies unique position: fashion house with genuine manufacture capability, distinctive design language, and horological credibility. They've earned respect through investment and restraint uncommon among luxury houses entering watchmaking.
