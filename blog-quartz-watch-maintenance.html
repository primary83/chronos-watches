Quartz Watch Maintenance

January 2025 · 8 min read

Quartz watches are remarkably low-maintenance compared to their mechanical counterparts, but they're not maintenance-free. Here's what you really need to know to keep your quartz timepiece running accurately for years.

The Quartz Advantage

Quartz movements use a battery-powered oscillating quartz crystal to keep time with extraordinary accuracy—typically within 15 seconds per month. With fewer moving parts than mechanical watches, they require less frequent servicing and are more resistant to positional errors and shocks.

However, "low maintenance" doesn't mean "no maintenance." Neglecting your quartz watch can lead to problems that are easily preventable.

Battery Replacement: The Critical Task

The most important maintenance task for any quartz watch is timely battery replacement. Most quartz watch batteries last 2-5 years, depending on the movement and complications. Some high-end quartz movements can run up to 10 years on a single battery.

Many quartz watches have an End-of-Life (EOL) indicator—the second hand starts jumping in 4-second intervals instead of ticking every second. When you see this, replace the battery promptly.

Why Prompt Battery Replacement Matters

  • Dead batteries can leak acid and damage the movement
  • Gaskets are checked and replaced during battery service
  • Delays can lead to more expensive repairs
  • Water resistance is compromised when the case is opened improperly

Who Should Replace the Battery?

For fashion watches and basic quartz timepieces, a reputable jeweler or watch shop can handle battery replacement. However, for luxury quartz watches or any watch with significant water resistance ratings, always use an authorized service center.

Opening a watch case requires proper tools and a clean environment. Improper battery replacement can introduce dust into the movement or compromise water resistance seals.

Water Resistance Maintenance

Even though quartz watches have fewer moving parts, their water resistance degrades over time just like mechanical watches. Gaskets dry out and lose elasticity. If your quartz watch is rated for water resistance, have the seals checked and pressure tested every 2-3 years—ideally at the same time as battery replacement.

Cleaning Your Quartz Watch

Regular cleaning keeps your quartz watch looking great. For water-resistant models, rinse under lukewarm water and use a soft toothbrush to clean the bracelet and case. Dry thoroughly with a lint-free cloth.

For non-water-resistant watches, use a slightly damp cloth on the case and a dry brush on the bracelet. Never submerge watches that aren't water-resistant, even for cleaning.

Do Quartz Watches Need Servicing?

While quartz watches don't need the frequent servicing of mechanical watches, they do benefit from occasional professional attention. Every 5-10 years, consider a full service that includes movement cleaning, gasket replacement, and case refinishing if needed.

High-end quartz movements (like those from Grand Seiko or Breitling SuperQuartz) may have specific service recommendations—check your manufacturer's guidelines.

Storage Tips

If storing a quartz watch for an extended period (more than a year), consider having the battery removed by a professional. This eliminates any risk of battery leakage. Store in a cool, dry place away from magnets and extreme temperatures.

For watches you wear occasionally, simply ensure the battery is fresh and wear them periodically to keep gaskets from drying out in a compressed position.
