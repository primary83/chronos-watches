About Garmin

Founded in 1989 by Gary Burrell and Min Kao, Garmin began as an aviation GPS company. The brand entered the fitness wearables market in 2003 with the Forerunner 101, establishing itself as the serious athlete's choice for GPS tracking.

Garmin is beloved by runners, triathletes, hikers, and pilots for its best-in-class GPS accuracy, comprehensive training metrics, and legendary battery life. While other smartwatches measure battery in hours, Garmin measures in weeks—and with solar models, potentially forever.

Key innovations include multi-band GPS for superior accuracy, solar charging, Body Battery energy monitoring, and Training Status features that help athletes optimize recovery and performance.
