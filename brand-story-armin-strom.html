The History of Armin Strom

January 2025 · 9 min read

Armin Strom built his reputation skeletonizing watches for other brands before creating his own. Today, the manufacture bearing his name produces entirely in-house movements featuring resonance technology and the distinctive visible mechanics that made him famous.

The Master Skeletonizer

Armin Strom (the person) began as a watchmaker specializing in skeletonization—the art of removing material from movements to reveal their inner workings. His skill became legendary; he skeletonized pieces for major brands and private collectors alike.

In 2006, he founded the brand bearing his name to create complete watches rather than modify others'.

Biel/Bienne Manufacture

Armin Strom's manufacture in Biel/Bienne, Switzerland, handles everything from movement construction to case production. Complete vertical integration allows control over every aspect of their watches—unusual even among "manufacture" brands.

Resonance

Armin Strom's Mirrored Force Resonance represents their most significant technical achievement. Two balance wheels, connected only through resonance (vibration transfer through the movement), synchronize to improve accuracy. This physical phenomenon, first explored by Christiaan Huygens in the 17th century, finds elegant modern expression in Armin Strom's interpretation.

Skeleton DNA

The brand's DNA remains skeletonization—but executed differently. Rather than removing material from existing movements, Armin Strom designs movements specifically for visibility. Every component is shaped, finished, and positioned for visual impact.

Gravity Equal Force

The Gravity Equal Force addresses a fundamental issue: mainspring tension decreases as it unwinds, affecting accuracy. Armin Strom's solution delivers constant force to the escapement regardless of mainspring state. Technical innovation meeting practical improvement.

Distinctive Aesthetics

Armin Strom watches have unmistakable visual identity: exposed mechanics, bold finishing in the brand's signature colors, and design that celebrates rather than hides the movement. They look technical without being cold.

Edge Collection

The Edge collection offers Armin Strom character at relatively accessible pricing—"relative" meaning still luxury, but below their high complications. These pieces bring the brand's aesthetic and in-house movements to broader audiences.

Recognition

Multiple Grand Prix d'Horlogerie de Genève nominations and wins validate Armin Strom's technical and aesthetic achievements. The independent watchmaking community recognizes them as genuine innovators.

Armin Strom Today

The brand continues developing new movements and complications while maintaining the visible-mechanics philosophy that defines them. For collectors who want to see their watch work—not just tell time—Armin Strom offers compelling options at various price points.
