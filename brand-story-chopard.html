The History of Chopard

January 2, 2025 · 11 min read

"The Artisan of Emotions." Chopard creates timepieces and jewelry designed to evoke joy and celebration. From the playful Happy Diamonds to the prestigious Cannes Film Festival partnership, Chopard embodies luxury with heart—and has become a leader in ethical sourcing.

The Founding

In 1860, Louis-Ulysse Chopard established his workshop in Sonvilier, Switzerland. His specialty was pocket watches for European railway companies. By the 1960s, the founding family was aging, and German goldsmith Karl Scheufele purchased the brand in 1963, relocating to Geneva.

Happy Diamonds

In 1976, Chopard introduced Happy Diamonds—watches featuring loose diamonds floating freely between two sapphire crystals. This revolutionary concept became Chopard's signature, spawning entire collections that define the brand's joyful personality.

"We have the freedom to follow our hearts. That's the privilege of being family-owned." — Caroline Scheufele

L.U.C Manufacture

In 1996, Chopard opened its own movement manufacture in Fleurier—the L.U.C line. These calibers have won multiple Grand Prix d'Horlogerie awards, proving Chopard can compete with traditional manufacture brands on pure watchmaking merit.

Cannes Film Festival

Since 1998, Chopard creates the Palme d'Or trophy and adorns stars on the red carpet. The brand has become synonymous with cinema's most glamorous moments.

Ethical Leadership

By 2018, Chopard committed to using 100% ethical gold in all production—setting a standard few competitors have matched. The Green Carpet Collection proves luxury and responsibility can coexist.

Modern Collections

Happy Sport: Steel meets dancing diamonds—accessible luxury with playful spirit.
L.U.C: Haute horlogerie with in-house movements and sophisticated complications.
Alpine Eagle: Luxury steel sports watch competing with Royal Oak and Nautilus.
Mille Miglia: Racing-inspired chronographs for the legendary Italian road rally.

Still family-owned, Chopard proves independent luxury can thrive. The Scheufele family has created an artisan of emotions that honors heritage while building something Louis-Ulysse Chopard never could have imagined.
