The History of Bremont

January 2025 · 9 min read

Bremont is genuinely British—not a British brand using Swiss manufacturing, but a company building watches in England with British engineering. Their military connections aren't marketing; they're real partnerships with elite forces who depend on these watches.

Brothers and Aviation

Nick and Giles English founded Bremont in 2002, naming the company after a French farmer who helped their father after an aviation accident. Both brothers are pilots, and their father was an aeronautical engineer—aviation runs deep in Bremont's DNA.

This wasn't a fashion brand entering watches; it was aviation enthusiasts creating timepieces they'd trust in the cockpit.

Testing Beyond Standards

Bremont subjects watches to their "Trip-Tick" testing—a proprietary process exceeding industry standards. Watches endure extreme temperatures, pressure changes, vibration, and impacts that simulate real aviation conditions. This testing isn't theoretical; it validates watches for actual flight use.

Military Partnerships

Bremont has developed watches for elite military units including the Royal Navy, British Army, and special forces. The Martin-Baker collection—created with the ejection seat manufacturer—is available only to pilots who have ejected using Martin-Baker seats. You can't buy your way into that club.

These military partnerships provide real-world validation that marketing cannot fabricate.

British Manufacturing

Bremont has invested heavily in British manufacturing capability, opening "The Wing" facility in Henley-on-Thames. They produce cases, dials, and components in England—unusual when most brands outsource everything to Switzerland or Asia.

Their goal is increasing British content in each watch, rebuilding manufacturing expertise that Britain largely lost.

The MBII and MBIII

The Martin-Baker collection represents Bremont's most distinctive offering. These pilot watches feature ejection seat-tested cases, unique serial numbers tied to pilots' ejection records, and design elements honoring aviation heritage.

Supermarine Collection

The Supermarine collection offers dive watches named after the company that built the Spitfire. These robust divers provide serious water resistance while maintaining Bremont's aviation-inspired aesthetic.

SOLO and Airco

The SOLO provides entry to Bremont at lower price points, while the Airco collection honors de Havilland aircraft with vintage-inspired designs.

Movement Development

Bremont has developed in-house movements, though they also use modified Swiss calibers. Their ENG300 series movements demonstrate serious manufacturing ambition beyond simple assembly.

Royal Connection

Bremont holds a Royal Warrant and has created pieces for significant British occasions. This royal recognition validates their position as Britain's premier watchmaker.

Bremont Today

Bremont offers something genuinely different: British-made watches with real aviation and military credentials. For buyers who value authenticity over marketing, British engineering over Swiss convention, Bremont provides timepieces with substance behind the story.
