Buying Watches

What is an authorized dealer?
An authorized dealer has a direct contractual relationship with the watch brand. They receive watches directly from the manufacturer, are trained by the brand, and can offer official warranties. Purchasing from authorized dealers guarantees authenticity. Learn more in our guide: Why Buy From Authorized Dealers.
What's the difference between authorized and grey market?
Authorized dealers have brand approval and offer manufacturer warranties. Grey market dealers sell genuine watches but without brand authorization, typically at discounted prices—but without manufacturer warranty coverage. We only link to authorized sources.
How can I verify if a dealer is authorized?
Visit the brand's official website and use their dealer locator tool. You can also contact the brand directly to confirm a retailer's status. All retailers featured on CHRONOS have been verified as authorized.
Why are some watches so hard to buy?
Certain models from brands like Rolex, Patek Philippe, and Audemars Piguet have demand that far exceeds supply. Authorized dealers often maintain waiting lists, and allocation may depend on purchase history. This scarcity is real—be wary of anyone offering these watches without a wait.
What's my first luxury watch should be?
It depends on your budget, style, and intended use. Generally, we recommend starting with versatile models from established brands like Omega, Tudor, or TAG Heuer. Read our complete guide: Your First Luxury Watch.

Watch Care & Service

How often should I service my watch?
Most manufacturers recommend service every 5-7 years for mechanical watches. Quartz watches need battery replacement every 2-3 years. Always use authorized service centers to maintain warranty coverage and ensure genuine parts.
Can any watchmaker service my luxury watch?
While many independent watchmakers are skilled, using non-authorized service can void your warranty and potentially affect resale value. For watches under warranty, always use the brand's authorized service network. Read more: Watch Maintenance Guide.
How do I spot a fake watch?
Common signs include weight differences, poor finishing, misspellings, incorrect fonts, and movement quality. However, counterfeits are increasingly sophisticated. The safest approach is buying from authorized sources. See our detailed guide: How to Spot a Fake Luxury Watch.
